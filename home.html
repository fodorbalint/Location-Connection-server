Location Connection
For professional singles
Location Connection is a free dating app, which combines simplicity with functionality.

Key features:
Find people without registration
It is needed for interaction only.
Choose from people who liked you
You can filter people by relation, such as:
  • everyone
  • people you neither liked or hid
  • those you liked, you matched with, added as friend or hid
  • users who liked or hid you
When a like is mutual, you can chat.
Search everywhere
You can narrow the list of people by using location and setting a maximum distance. The center point can be either your location, or an address you set.
Users are also searchable by their name, username or keywords in their descriptions.
Simple registration
You need to provide a picture, a name and a bio.
It is not required to specify age, height, weight or other things, but if someone does mention it in their bios, you can find them using number range search.
Be in control of your visibility
Your last active date, response rate or register date will determine if you appear on the top of the lists. The sort order can be reversed though, so watch out for your statistics.
Share your location
Make your location public, or share it only with people you liked, you matched with, or added as friend. The same goes with distance.
If you do not use own location, your profile will still show up for others if they do not filter the results by distance.
Chat with certainty
You can see if your messages were read and when.
Except for situations where your match receives a background notification, messages will be marked seen or read depending on where your partner saw them.
Send location updates
This feature is available between matches. If you initiate location updates, your partner will see your location change on a map every 15-60 seconds depending on your settings.
Free, ad-free, and open-source
The costs associated with store presence, running the web server, and promotional campaigns are on me within my budget. To extend the app's reach, donations are welcome.
As a user, you should have access to the inner workings of the app. If you are ever in doubt about a function and somewhat familiar with programming languages, take a look at the source code.

About
I am Balint Fodor, the developer of Location Connection.

From early childhood I have been interested in making contact with the opposite sex, and with the advent of dating sites, I continued my quest of finding a girlfriend using the Internet.
I went on two dates in the early days - none of which resulted in a relationship; years later however I met a girl on the street whom I was together with for one and half years.
In or out of relationship, I continued my dating efforts, but even though smartphones and apps were invented, my life remained the same. I still got a date somehow, though it was a one-time occasion like 15 years before.

The hurdles you encounter on dating apps become clear when things do not go as expected.
Due to the business model that is based upon restricting features and keeping things secret, you might not get to meet the one you are supposed to. Add to it the other complexities and requirements, and you get a platform that is not in your favor, but which you use only because you do not have a better choice.

It was time to make a difference - and who else will do it than an ex-programmer with a personal interest in dating?
I started working on Location Connection on the 28 September 2019.
It was open for registration already a month later, but it took me 3 months to publish the app on Google Play, and the same amount of time was needed for the iOS version too. After 8 months in total, the platform was comparable to modern dating apps, and ready to be advertised.

You may now freely look through the registered profiles to see if there is anyone who interests you. Or create a profile, so others can find you later.
Whatever your use case is, do not hesitate to contact me if you run into any issues, have a question or suggestion.
